Projects

"Biocharge" Energy Harvesting Wear
Engineering Senior Design

Senior Design Capstone project with the goal of developing a wearable biomechanical energy harvesting device to charge an iPhone with a net zero carbon footprint. Narrowed down a list of biomechanical charging mechanisms through materials processing to the compression of piezo-electric crystals as an additive component of footwear. Sketched, designed, and reiterated prototypes using Solidworks, PLC Systems, and 3-D printed support structures. Successfully charged an iPhone 14 as part of a Senior Design Expo.

Predictive Analytics Dashboard
Maritime Security Center

Developed a predictive analytics dashboard with a multidisciplinary team of amazing engineers to provide insight on over 10 years of coastal incident data for the US Coast Guard. Applied statistical aggregation strategies and seasonality systems using Python to create an adaptable polar chart used to predict coastal incidents based on time of year. Presented a Tableau-based dashboard for the US Coast Guard to adopt new technologies and better allocate manpower for its 40,000+ active personnel.

(Illustration of an Electric Smart Meter, 2019)

Priority Meter Allocation Script
Con Edison AMI Project

Developed an algorithm to identify over 10,0000 priority meter banks for allocation to ConEdison technicians as part of the Advanced Meter Infrastructure (AMI) Project (2016-2021). Created an excel-based script that sorted Manhattan meter banks based on categorical and numerical properties of importance. Connected local script to ConEdison server database to provide reliable real time meter identification. Provided identified meter banks to technicians in crucial parts of Manhattan.

Studios

RedBoxStudios

Since I enjoy coding during my spare time, I decided to construct a video game. The goal was for it to be fun, yet educational. However, the reality is that coding by itself can be challenging and making it an interactive learning experience can be even more intricate. So I designed RedBoxStudios as a way to both challenge creativity and teach kids (or anyone else) the skill of coding through gaming. Currently, RedBoxStudios has over 4 open-source experiences on Roblox that enable kids to enjoy their free time while utilizing the tools provided for programming simple to complex software solutions.
